How it works

It watches so you don't have to.

No timers to start, no logs to fill in. FocusBook runs in your tray and does the accounting for you — then interrupts the spiral before it costs you an hour.

1

It sees the active window

Every few seconds FocusBook notes which app — and which browser tab — is in front of you. The tab reporter extension handles Chrome, Brave and Edge.

2

It sorts each one automatically

Rules map every app and site to a category and a verdict: Productive, Neutral or Distracting. Disagree? Reclassify once and it remembers.

3

It steps in when you drift

Slide from focused work into something distracting and a full-screen nudge appears — stay focused, take a short cooldown, or dismiss it for this app.

What's inside

Everything you need to protect your attention.

A focus tracker, a productivity ledger, and a gentle enforcer — in one desktop app that stays out of your way.

Automatic time tracking

Time is logged per app, per tab, per hour — no manual timers. See your day broken into productive, neutral and distracting minutes.

Productivity analytics

A dashboard with daily and weekly trends, a productivity score, and a per-app breakdown so you can see exactly what's eating your time.

Distraction blocking

When you slip into a distracting app mid-focus, a full-screen popup catches it — with cooldowns and per-app dismissals so it's firm, not annoying.

Custom categories

Your idea of "productive" is yours. Reassign any app or site to a category and productivity level, and FocusBook honors it from then on.

Optional AI insights

Plug in your own API key to get plain-language summaries of your habits and where your focus leaks — entirely optional, entirely under your control.

Private by design

All tracking runs and stays on your own machine. The browser extension reports only which tab is active — never durations, never your history.

Step 2 · Browser extension

Tab Reporter

Lets FocusBook see which browser tab is active so websites get tracked, not just apps. Works in Chrome, Brave and Edge from one codebase.

  • Reports the active tab — never durations
  • Talks to the app over a local, token-secured link
  • Included with the app — nothing extra to download
  1. Open chrome://extensions (or brave:// / edge://extensions) and turn on Developer mode.
  2. Click Load unpacked and pick the extension folder inside your FocusBook install.
  3. In FocusBook → Settings, copy the pairing token and paste it into the extension's Options page.

Questions

Good to know

Is FocusBook really free?
Yes. FocusBook is free and open source. There's no account, no subscription, and no paywalled features — download it and it's yours.
Where does my data go?
Nowhere but your own PC. All tracking data is stored locally in your user folder with automatic local backups. The browser extension only tells the app which tab is active over a local, token-authenticated connection — it never sends your browsing history or durations anywhere.
Which platforms are supported?
The installer ships for Windows 10 and 11. The browser extension works in Chrome, Brave and Microsoft Edge from a single codebase.
Do I need the browser extension?
The app tracks desktop applications on its own. Add the extension when you want website-level detail — so time on individual tabs (docs, YouTube, email) is categorized instead of lumped under "browser."
Is the AI feature required?
No. AI Insights is entirely optional and only runs if you add your own API key. FocusBook's tracking, analytics and blocking work fully without it.
Will it slow down my computer?
It's built to be light. FocusBook checks the active window on a slow interval and saves in the background, sitting quietly in your system tray while you work.
